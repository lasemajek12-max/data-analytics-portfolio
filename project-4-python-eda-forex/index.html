What the Data Revealed

📅
Revenue dips are seasonal, not random
Two consistent months per year showed revenue dips regardless of rep activity or product mix — pointing to market seasonality rather than internal performance issues.
🏆
Top rep outperforms bottom by 2.3× — but deal size is almost identical
The performance gap between the highest and lowest rep came entirely from trade volume, not deal quality. The bottom reps needed more pipeline, not bigger deals.
📉
Larger deals actually compress margins (r = −0.12)
Correlation analysis showed a weak but consistent negative relationship between deal size and profit margin — meaning the strategy of chasing large tickets was slightly counterproductive.
🌍
MENA: smallest region, fastest growth
Despite being the lowest-revenue region, MENA showed the steepest month-on-month growth trajectory — signalling where to prioritise expansion investment.

Sample — Rep Performance Ranking

📓 forex_trading_eda.ipynb · Section 05
# Sales rep performance — revenue, deal size, and target attainment

rep_performance = df.groupby('sales_rep').agg(
    total_revenue   = ('net_revenue',  'sum'),
    avg_deal_size   = ('net_revenue',  'mean'),
    total_trades    = ('trade_id',     'count'),
    target_revenue  = ('monthly_target','sum')
).reset_index()

rep_performance['attainment_pct'] = (
    rep_performance['total_revenue'] /
    rep_performance['target_revenue'] * 100
).round(1)

rep_performance = rep_performance.sort_values('total_revenue', ascending=True)

# Colour bars by attainment: green = above target, amber = near, red = below
colours = [
    '#6fcf97' if x >= 100 else
    '#f5a623' if x >= 85  else
    '#eb5757'
    for x in rep_performance['attainment_pct']
]

ax.barh(rep_performance['sales_rep'],
       rep_performance['total_revenue'],
       color=colours, edgecolor='none', height=0.65)
